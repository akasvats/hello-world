DR. A .P .J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, commonly known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He hailed from a humble background and overcame several challenges to become one of India's most renowned scientists and the 11th President of India. Kalam graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1958 with a degree in aerospace engineering. He played a significant role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts, earning him the title of the "Missile Man of India." He was instrumental in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and various missile systems. Apart from his contributions to science and technology, Kalam was also a prolific writer and an inspiring speaker. He was deeply passionate about igniting young minds and encouraging them to pursue their dreams. He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds." In 2002, Kalam was elected as the President of India, serving until 2007. During his presidency, he continued to focus on education, youth empowerment, and societal development. He was widely admired for his humility, integrity, and vision for a better India. After his presidential term, Kalam remained active in various capacities, continuing to inspire millions through his lectures and writings. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of scientific excellence, leadership, and humanitarianism.
